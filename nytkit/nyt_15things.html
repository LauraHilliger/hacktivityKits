Activity

Reading, Writing and Coding with The New York Times

Make a favorites list with HTML and CSS

Made by the National Writing Project

Step-by-step

  1. To begin, have students read The Legacy of an Author Lingers at the Met from The New York Times. Follow the steps below to write a two paragraph response to the article, then use Thimble to create a visual list of your favorite things--in this case, your favorite books!

  2. After reading the Times article explain why E.L. Konigsburg’s From the Mixed-up Files of Mrs. Basil E. Frankweiler will forever be remembered by many who visit the Metropolitan Museum of Art. Explain why this connection exists, using examples from the article. Then, in a second paragraph, share a place or location that you will always associate with a favorite book.

  3. Brainstorm a list of your favorite books and their settings. What makes them special? Pick one and be sure to include this information in your informative writing.

  4. Webmaker’s Top 15 Favorite Things activity allows students to show off 15 things they love on a Web site they create. For this activity, students should create a Top 15 list about books they love while experimenting with HTML and CSS to make a beautiful webpage.

  5. Use the Thimble app to create a list of your favorite books. Or you can get creative and list your favorite book covers, magazines, articles, or even your favorite authors!
