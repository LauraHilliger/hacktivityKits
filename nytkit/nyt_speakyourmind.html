Activity

Reading, Writing and Coding with The New York Times

Create Web pages with HTML and CSS

Made by the National Writing Project

Step-by-step

  1. To begin, have students read City Plan Sets 21 as Legal Age to Buy Tobacco from The New York Times. Follow the steps below to write a letter to New York's health commissioner then use your writing as the basis for a Web page using HTML and CSS.

  2. Should the legal age to buy tobacco be raised to 21 or remain at 18 in New York City? Write a letter to New York City’s health commissioner making an argument for or against the change. Use evidence from the text in your answer. Decide which side of the argument you fall on and craft a clear statement of purpose summarizing the issue and reasons for your position.

  3. A crucial step in creating interesting Web pages is learning CSS, or Cascading Style Sheets, which enables creators to change fonts, colors, graphics, and many other aspects of design. Mozilla’s Speak Your Mind project teaches students how to use CSS, along with HTML, to create Web pages that allow them to experiment with writing voice and technology at the same time.

  4. It's time to create a Web page based on your letter using Speak Your Mind! After writing your letter to the commissioner follow the instructions and use Thimble to create a Web page based on your writing. On your Web page you can support New York's decision or criticize it.

  5. What makes a Web page attractive? Make sure you use images and text that are attractive and get your point across.
