Activity

Make a movie pitch

Come up with your own movie idea. Then create a web page to promote it.

Created by National Writing Project

Description

In this activity, you'll learn how to create a simple web page that shows off your creativity and persuasive writing skills. After reading an article about the growing movie market in China, you'll come up with a movie idea of your own -- then create a web pages that "sells" it to Hollywood executives.

This activity is part of a larger lesson plan called "Coding with the New York Times"


Get started

  1. To begin, read "U.S. Box Office Heroes Proving Mortal in China" from The New York Times. Then, watch the related video clip.
  2. Now think of some popular movies you like. What do those movies have in common?
  3. Make a list of popular movie genres and choose your favorite.
  4. Do some research on some recent box office returns. (You might try a Google search.) Determine what makes these movies successful.
  5. Now imagine that you are a Chinese movie executive tasked with finding the next big hit. The twist: your superiors want you to find a movie that will be a big hit in your country, too. Come up with an exciting, detailed and convincing movie pitch that you think would be “greenlit” by executives convinced it would work for home audiences, too.
  6. After writing the pitch, it's time to take it online! Go to the Webmaker "Your Movie Pitch" project. Hit the green "remix" button in the top right corner of the page.

  7. Now use Thimble to change the code and make your own page. To do this, you'll need to play around with the code on the left-hand side. The code is written in a language called HTML. Try changing things like the text, title and background image to make it your own.
  8. If you're looking for some good pictures or graphics to use, you could try searching for Creative Commons photos here.

Discussion

What are the ingredients of a winning movie pitch?

How did you make your idea compelling for both audiences in China and in your home country?

What did you include on your page to encourage a Hollywood executive to greenlight your idea?

Is your web page easy to read and look at? Does it tell your story well?
