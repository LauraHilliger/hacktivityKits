Activity

Web Literacy vs Digital Literacy

Unpack the topic of Web Literacy and allow learners to plan and create an interactive project to reflect on what they know about Web Literacy vs Digital Literacy

Made by Laura

Steps for the Activity

  1. Break into groups: Have groups make a list or a drawing of what they know about Web Literacy and Digital Literacy. Have them think about these questions and make a plan:

    • Why is Web Literacy unique?
    • What are the practical barriers to implementation?
    • What are the opportunities that connect web literacy to other areas/learning?

    Have participants brainstorm what their projects will be. You may want to place some creative constraints to help people get making faster. Provide your learners with the Designing for the Web: Planning resource, which will help them place their own constraints. Be sure and provide them with the HTML and CSS Cheatsheets!

  2. Start building! Have learners find media that can serve as the visuals for their reflections (try the Connected Learning and NWP's DigitalIs sites).

  3. Have learners use Thimble to create their reflections and/or stories. Allow them to collaborate and tinker around with their own ideas to figure out what they want to make.

Materials

  • Markers
  • Paper
  • Computer
  • Web Access
  • Etherpad
  • HTML Cheatsheet
  • CSS Basic Cheatsheet

Discussion

Save 20 to 30 minutes at the end of the two hours to have participants talk about their projects and display the work they have done. Following the presentations, facilitators should direct participants to other resources in their community or online to learn more about making and remixing. Provide individual constructive feedback during presentations.
