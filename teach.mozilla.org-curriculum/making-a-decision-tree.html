Activity

Making a Decision Tree

Making a decision tree is a good way to think about specific factors that would influence a specific decision. They are also useful for tracing patterns and pathways that might lead to a certain kind of decision. In this activity, learners will draw a decision tree on paper.

Made by Laura

    Find your topic

  1. Before we start drawing decision trees, we need to have a decision to make. Divide learners into groups and give them 10 or 15 minutes to talk about what they want their story to be about. Give them design constraints (e.g. Your story needs to be about a historical event) or use an activity like Adventure time to get them thinking about particular characters and situations. Show some examples of fun decision trees.

    2. Define the decisions

  2. Once learners have a topic, ask them to define the first decision the user will have to make. On a big piece of paper, have learners draw and label a square to represent the decision. Next, have learners draw out lines for each possible answer, and write that answer along the line. Have learners keep spacing between lines so that they can expand their thoughts.
  3. Have learners review their possible answers and outline what decision a user will have to make IF they choose the first possible answer, THEN what is the next decision a user will make? Repeat this step for each possible answer.
  4. Have learners continue to refine their decision trees. Tell them that "Nothing Happens" is an indication that that "branch" of the tree is finished.

    5. Start Brainstorming

  5. Once the paper decision trees are finished, have learners start brainstorming about types of media they might build into a digital version. Then check out the Appmaker activity and have learners build their own Choose Your Own Adventure stories.

Materials

  • Paper
  • Pens or markers
  • Decision tree examples

Discussion

In programming, a conditional is a statement that performs different computations or actions. Many languages support IF/THEN statements, conditional statements that returns a value as a result. How are IF/THEN statements represented in your decision trees?
