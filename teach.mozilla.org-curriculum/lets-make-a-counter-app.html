Let's Make a Counter App!

This activity serves as an introduction to app-making with Appmaker and guides a learner through the interface while helping them make a simple counter app.

Made by Bobby

Useful background information

Appmaker is an online tool that lets you drag and drop different components, called "Bricks" to easily build fun and useful mobile apps. It's the newest tool in the Webmaker collection, and we're eager for feedback!

The goal of this tutorial is to understand how Appmaker Bricks, the pre-programmed building blocks Appmaker uses to provide specific pieces of functionality, work and to guide you through the process of designing an app with Appmaker.

A finished version of the app we'll build is available for reference (and remixing!) here.

What we're building

We're going to build a simple counter app.

Let's get started.

Open the Appmaker Designer at http://apps.webmaker.org/designer and explore the interface. On the left, there is a tray with the various "Bricks" Appmaker has preprogrammed, in the middle you'll see your working space, and on the right you'll see the customization panel.

Get Started

  1. Add a Button

    Click "Button" in the Brick tray to add a Button brick. Bricks are functional as soon as they've been added to your project.

    Click the button a few times on the phone in your working space. Whenever the button is clicked, it sends out a "Click!" message. We can customize which brick "hears" that message to and initiate a corresponding reaction.

    The Button option in the Brick tray.

    A Button Brick inside our mobile app.

    The "Click" message being sent from the Button Brick whenever it's clicked.

  2. Add a Counter

    Click "Counter" in the Brick tray. Your app should now have a Button Brick stacked on top of a Counter Brick.

    Here's where the magic happens. Click on the Button Brick a few more times, and watch what happens. The "Click!" message travels from the Button Brick to the Counter Brick. When the Button's message is received by the Counter, the Counter responds by increasing its count.

    An app with two Bricks: a Button and a Counter.

    The Counter Brick receiving a message from the Button Brick.

  3. Add another Button

    Counting up is fun, but what about counting down?

    Click on "Button" in the Brick tray again, and another Button will be added to our app. Unfortunately, though, both Buttons have the same effect: they both cause the Counter to count up. We need to change some logic!

    With your mouse, hover over the right edge of the second Button Brick, and click on the menu that pops up. When the colour picker appears, choose orange.

    Another Button!

    Hovering over the right edge of a Button Brick.

    The message colour picker.

    Clicking on the second Button Brick has no effect on the Counter. First, we need to tell the Counter Brick to "listen" for orange messages.

    Use your mouse to hover over the left side of the Counter Brick. Click on "Count Down" to reveal the colour picker, and choose orange again. There are now two colour pipes attached to the Counter Brick: blue for "Count Down", and orange for "Count Up".

    Counter Brick's message colour picker.

    Two coloured messages for the Counter Brick.

    Now, clicking on the second Button Brick sends an orange message to the Counter Brick telling it to "Count Down". Meanwhile, clicking on the first Button Brick still sends a blue message to the Counter Brick telling it to "Count Up".

    Counting down furiously!

    Counting up furiously!

  4. Bonus Round

    Click on the "Sign In" button in the top-right of Appmaker Designer. If you already have a Persona-enabled account, this process will be familiar to you. Otherwise, when a window pops up, follow the instructions to sign up for a Persona account or connect an existing account (e.g. GMail). Once you've signed in, you can save and publish your app. Click on the "App" menu, and choose "Save As". When prompted, type in a name for your app. Notice the title of your app appear on the phone display. Next, click on the same menu and choose "Publish".
