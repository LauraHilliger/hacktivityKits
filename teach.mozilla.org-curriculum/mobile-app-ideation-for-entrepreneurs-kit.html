Mobile App Ideation for Entrepreneurs Kit

Participants will brainstorm to identify problems and design opportunities based on real world circumstances while they learn about the basics of mobile devices and working openly.

Made by Laura with huge thanks to Jess, Kat and Bobby.

Description

All entrepreneurs need an idea, but first they need to have a problem to solve. In this workshop participants will explore basics of mobile technology and brainstorm to identify problems and design opportunities based on real world circumstances.

They will learn what it means to be working openly, and start to see themselves as having the necessary skills for entrepreneurship. This workshop is designed to encourage confidence, collaboration and give participants agency to act on their ideas.

Participants will:

  • discuss and reflect on mobile basics and technology
  • identify a real world problem
  • get inspired to take action
  • work together to develop ideas
  • consider how mobile technology can be part of the solution
  • paper prototype an app idea
  • make a roadmap

Big Ideas

  • Defining a problem is the first step in focused idea development
  • Design is a response to an inquiry or a problem
  • Paper prototypes act as blueprints for app implementation
  • Systems can be broken down into parts ( components, core mechanics, goals, rules, space and choice).
  • Inspiration and ideas can come from anywhere.
  • Designing for a mobile experience provides unique affordances
  • User testing allows you to see what improvements need to be made to your design in real time.
  • Brainstorming helps you to work with peers to kickstart an idea or concept for a design.

Agenda

  1. [20 mins] Introduction and Mobile Basics
  2. [20 mins] Storytime on the Go
  3. [25 mins] Defining the Problem
  4. [40 mins] Firestarter
  5. [40 mins] Mobile App Brainstorming and Speed Geeking
  6. [40 mins] Paper Prototyping Mobile Apps
  7. [45 mins] Roadmapping My Next Steps
  8. [15 mins] Closing Circle and Reflections

Learning objectives

  • Gain an understanding as to what a problem to solve might be
  • Formulate solution statements
  • Explore a variety of methodologies for brainstorming
  • Develop confidence around idea sharing and reflection
  • Learn to collaborate around ideas and prototypes
  • Evaluate prototypes and plan next steps

The concrete Web Literacy skills that will be developed are in the domains of Building and Connecting

Essential Questions

  • How does defining the problem well affect your design decisions?
  • Is a mobile app appropriate for your solution?
  • Did you paper prototype translate into an application?

Assessment and review

You can get an idea of whether learners have gained from this session by noticing if a learner:

  • Demonstrates knowledge gained through hands-on activities, games or discussion.
  • Works collaboratively and openly with others to complete activities or tasks.
  • Uses creative methods to build ideas and plans in novel and unexpected ways.
