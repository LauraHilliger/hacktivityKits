Teaching Kit

Make. Hack. Play. at NWP Annual Meeting

Get Creative and Share! 3 Hour Agenda for a session at the NWP Annual Meeting

Made by: Laura Hilliger

Description

This teaching kit is designed to get folks interacting with one another and thinking outside of the box in terms of how we #teachtheweb and what we can do with Webmaker tools. It was made to bring NWP Educators up to speed on Mozilla, the Webmaker tools, and the NWP + Mozilla partnership.

Learning objectives

Participants will learn new participatory and collaborative activities and games. As well as:

  • how to access the Webmaker tools
  • how to use the Webmaker tools
  • why and how NWP and Webmaker are working together
  • that the Web Literacy Standard exists
  • how to get involved
Make. Hack. Play. was a collaborative event created by:
  • Mozilla Webmaker (@Webmaker)
  • the National Writing Project (@NWPDigital_Is)

What you'll make together

Participants will work together to make Webmaker Makes and mini makes to teach reading and writing in a digital world.

Preparation

Modern browsers should be installed on any computers you'll be using. We also set up an Etherpad for this session - you should too!

Assessment and review

  • Discussion questions:
    • What excited you the most?
    • the least?
    • How will you take what you made in this workshop further?
    • How do you imagine implementing what you learned today in your classrooms/schools?
  • Assessment: I'm asking that participants give their peers written feedback in the form of a blog post. Don't just cover the workshop, cover something one of your peers did, a way he/she participated, what inspired you?
  • Sharing: #teachtheweb is a great tag to share work on Twitter.

Agenda

  1. 15 mins - Introductions & Setup
  2. 15 mins - Evolution for Geeks
  3. 15 mins - Synchronized Dancing (+Q&A)
  4. 2.25 hours - Make a Mini Maker's Scrum
