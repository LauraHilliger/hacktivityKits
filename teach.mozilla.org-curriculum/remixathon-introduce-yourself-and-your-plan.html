Activity

Remix-a-thon: Introduce Yourself and Your Plan

Warm up learners with an organic, interactive design process for webmaking. Participants play with the X-Ray Goggles to introduce themselves and set a goal for the future.

Made by Laura

Steps for the Activity

  1. [5 mins] Break into Groups. No one has to work alone! Try to keep your groups to 4 people or less, so that each participant has a chance to share their thoughts. Give each group a stack of post-it notes and a marker or pen.

  2. [10 minutes] Have learners collectively explore types of actions they might take with their local community. If you wish, you can use these questions to guide their discussions. Be sure to have learners document their conversation:

    • Who in your community do you care about teaching? Why?
    • What skills or knowledge do you want to share with them?
    • How would this knowledge benefit them?
    • How can you share this experience with your peers?
    • How do you envision sharing webmaking in your local context? Weekly workshops? An after school club? What's your ideal setting?
    • What challenges do you see with sharing webmaking?
    • What other topics will you use webmaking to address?
    • How is your vision unique?

  4. [15 minutes] Play with the X-Ray Goggles to document initial thoughts: Have learners work together to remix this page to introduce their group's members and their initial thoughts.

Materials

  • XRay Goggles installed
  • Computer
  • Web Access
  • A blog or Etherpad for posting work and/or links

Discussion

Have participants talk about their ideas and display the work they have done.

Following the presentations, facilitators should direct participants to other resources in their community or online to learn more about hacking and web design/development. Provide individual constructive feedback during presentations.
