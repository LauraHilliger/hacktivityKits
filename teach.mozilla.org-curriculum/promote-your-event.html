Event Kit

Promote your Event

Throw a super unique Maker Party by inviting a group of people together and using this freestyle technique to see what people want to make. This resource will show you how!

Made by: MozTeach

  • Create Talking Points

    You and your core planning team will want to speak publicly about the event. You'll want to communicate your message using consistent language. Craft 5-10 sentences or ideas that help you to explain the focus, structure and goals of your event. Here are some sample talking points to use [link] — feel free to borrow or put in your own words. Once you have your 5 - 10 talking points, share them with your peers and get feedback. Photo by Dron Rathore

  • Leverage Social Media

    Set up a Site

    Share your planning process and ideas widely. Create a page for your events - it's a great way to keep in touch with the global community!

    Define a Hashtag

    Create a hashtag for your event or use #makerparty and/or #teachtheweb. Throughout the event tweet and post on social networks.

    Get creative and make your own buzz

    Share what you are up to with posts to social media sites!

  • Craft a News Release

    A news release is a one-page document describing your event. Reporters refer to news releases when writing stories. Include links to websites and other resources connected to your event. Remember to include clear contact information. Photo by Mozilla Phillipines

  • Beef up your Contacts

    Send out the news release at least a week before the event. Notify Mozilla Webmaker as well as newspapers, TV and radio in your community — think about other outlets and organizations that potentially could spread the word about your event. This might be parent groups, techie meet-ups, bloggers, podcasters, tweeters, etc. Photo by Mozilla Phillipines
