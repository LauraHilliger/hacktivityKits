Teaching Kit

Teach the Web as NCS Digital Champions with Webmaker and NCS

This Teaching Kit is designed to support the first generation of NCS Digital Champions through their induction to their new role – the two day, in-person train the trainer workshop is hosted for NCS Trust by Mozilla.

Made by: Laura

Description

This Teaching Kit is designed to inspire and empower the first ever group of NCS Digital Champions as they begin their roles. As well as introducing them to their fellow champions and telling them more about what NCS and Mozilla expects of them as NCS Digital Champions, we aim to build their trust and confidence in live events and demystify Webmaker facilitation techniques.

We hope by the end of the weekend all 40 NCS Digital Champions will be equipped and excited about designing their own participatory, learner-centric events for their fellow NCS graduates and their wider community.

Over the course of the training, the NCS Digital Champions will learn how to see themselves as part of a community champions, how to build online and face-to-face activities for specific audiences and how to support each other. Mozilla will help the champions create their own event agendas and document what they plan on doing to share their digital skills in their local area, and right across the country.

Learning objectives

By the end of the weekend, NCS Digital Champions will be able to:

  • use the web in support of their ongoing social action
  • facilitate making as learning
  • run events, to share their learning with fellow NCS graduates and wider communities
  • make things with Webmaker tools
  • help others use Webmaker tools
  • document learning and event materials using templates
  • give/receive constructive criticism in the digital space
  • use critique to improve their work
  • collaborate and share online in a responsible way
  • understand and explain to others how to live their lives online
  • support NCS Trust in developing NCS online and social activities

The NCS Digital Champions will understand that:

  • they’re part of a national network of NCS Digital Champions and a wider global community that can help you promote your local initiatives
  • they’re key ambassadors for NCS online and in their communities
  • their digital skills can really help them, their fellow NCS graduates and NCS participants to support and promote their social action projects
  • collaborative participatory learning experiences are about community and learner focused design
  • facilitating and pacing the event is essential to sustaining participant engagement and energy levels
  • we have cultural notions of what learning is supposed to look like
  • the Web is made out of building blocks that they can rearrange to create new things
  • parts of a webpage include HTML, CSS, graphic assets and text content
  • we can add the teaching and learning of web literacy competencies into any subject or topic
  • working openly means you release early and often to improve your own practice
  • webmaking intermingles social skills, technical skills and topical knowledge
  • being part of a community means you can improve your practice and help improve other’s as well
  • inviting people into your work is a good way to give them agency to contribute

What we'll make together

We'll use all three of Mozilla's Webmaker tools to help the NCS Digital Champions think about their local initiatives and support them to understand how their creative energy can spur innovation within their network. We'll make:

  • An X-Ray Goggles remix of a site to include initial thoughts on event type, target audience, logistics and potential partners.
  • A Thimble event invitation and promotional page.
  • A Popcorn video about what participants have learned about facilitation.
  • A Teaching Kit with several activities and supporting media.
  • Creation of usable content and assets for promoting NCS

Preparation

You need a large room that can comfortably fit all your participants in a circle. Small break out rooms are a plus. Optional: a projector to show demos.

It is also recommended to have a team of co-facilitators who can help shape and run the agenda with you. We recommend 1 facilitator to 5 participants.

Assessment and review

  • Discussion questions:
    • How can your #teachtheweb work connect to your experiences of NCS, and your role as an NCS Digital Champion?
    • How can you adapt a resource and open it to other NCS graduates, and different people in your community? How can you hack your learning space to inspire more open learning?
    • How can remix invite learning?
    • Why is it important to connect to global communities?
    • How does communication online differ from communication offline?
    • What excited you the most about this training?
    • the least?
    • Sharing. Have you posted your work to the Webmaker G+ Community? Did you tweet with the #teachtheweb hashtag? Are you a member of the Webmaker Newsgroup?

Assessment criteria

Did the learner:
  • participate?
  • collaborate?
  • help others?
  • outline a reasonable Maker Party plan?
  • make an open educational resource?
  • try to make things with Webmaker tools?
