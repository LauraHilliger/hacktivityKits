Maker Party

How to Host a 1 hour Net Neutrality Maker Party

A small Maker Party is a fun way to spend an hour exploring the Web as a platform for change. Gather your family, friends or interested participants and explore Net Neutrality and take action to protect the open web.

See more Net Neutrality activities in the full Net Neutrality Teaching Kit

Made by Laura Hilliger, Michelle Thorne, Kat Braybrooke, Jess Klein, Chad Sansing, with thanks to Dave Steer

Steps for the Event

  1. Pick a date and schedule your party. Document what you intend to make and invite others to the party. Don't forget to include any mentors or co-organizers who helped with your event, so they get credit for teaching the web with you.

    Add your event

  2. Gather Friends and Family. Find a cozy space like your kitchen, living room or perhaps a nearby coffee shop. Set up your computer -- although you don't even need it or internet access to teach the web!

  3. Make something in support of Net Neutrality. Work together to design a poster, make a meme or run a video jam in support of Net Neutrality. You'll explore the topic of Net Neutrality while you are making!

  4. Share What You Made. Remix this report out to include a picture of YOUR Net Neutrality make. Add your projects and photos to your event page to tell a story about what you made. Share your event in the community discussion forum.

Details

  • 1 - 5 participants
  • 1 hour duration, no prep
  • In your kitchen or living room

Materials

  • Camera
  • Craft supplies
  • Paper
  • Computer
  • Web Access
  • Webmaker account(s)
  • Photo-sharing account(s)
