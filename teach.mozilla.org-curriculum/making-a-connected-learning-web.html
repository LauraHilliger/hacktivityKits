Activity

Making a Connected Learning Web

Learners explore and discuss making, connecting and learning through creating a dynamic web of string. The facilitator should think of this activity as a guided discussion, allow learners to explore themes while guiding them if their answers do not make sense.

Made by Laura

Steps for the Activity

  1. To set up the activity, get all participants standing in a circle. Explain that you hold the yarn to speak, and invite people to end their statement with a question.

  2. Holding onto one end of the string or yarn, toss the ball to someone in the group and ask them a question concerning Connected Learning. During the discussion, try to use Connected Learning terminology when talking about similar themes. Some sample questions might be:

    • What do you find important for learning and why?
    • What does Connected Learning mean to you?
    • Why is peer support important in learning?
    • How does interest affect your learning?
    • How have you integrated stuff you've learned for fun into your professional life?
    • What have you learned through making?
    • How does the web support this kind of learning?
    • How is the World Wide Web different from the other mass mediums?

  3. Whomever catches the ball gives their answer to the question and then makes a statement or asks a new question. Then, the learner tosses the ball to someone new.

  4. Continue the discussion until everyone has answered at least one question.

Materials

  • Ball of string or yarn

Discussion

Discuss how would the created web might look different if the questions, participants, etc had been in a different order.

Use the created web as a metaphor for the World Wide Web and discuss how we are all connected through information, cultural understandings and shared history.
