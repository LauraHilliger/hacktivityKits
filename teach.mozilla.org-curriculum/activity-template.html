Step by Step

Explain each step of the activity so that others can repeat it in their learning situations. Replace the example text and find images that help you explain your steps!

  1. An introductory activity gets the blood pumping, forms connections between learners, and introduces a topic. There are thousands of different ways to introduce a topic and get learners interacting with each other. Choose one and hack it to be your own!

  2. A good second activity is an explanatory activity, one that allows participants to get used to tools or procedures. An example would having very little design constraints and inviting the participants make something with Thimble or Popcorn. Before learners can practically apply new skills to solve a problem, they need to be comfortable with the tools they'll be using and have developed basic understandings of the skills they'll be sharpening.

  3. A practical, project based exploratory activity allows learners to delve deeper into the essential questions of your session. This activity should tie the introduction and explanation together, allowing the learner to develop deeper understandings. Go back to thinking of the results: what will your learners MAKE when they are done? What research do they need to do? What text, images, videos, sound clips do they need to gather?

  4. Make sure and find or create any resources your learners might need on creating content. Create a project that gives learners enough time to explore and tinker while working towards the completion of the activity.
