Teaching Kit

Prepare for your in-person Webmaker Training!

This kit is designed to help you prepare for an in-person workshop. The kit includes resources and tasks that you can read, make and start thinking about prior to the workshop.

Made by Laura

Description

You are invited to start your training online by completing the actions below. These actions shouldn't take more than an hour a day, and are designed to get you set up and thinking about concepts we will explore in person.

It looks like a lot, but it's not! Completing these actions will save us all valuable in-person time, of which there is never enough. Complete the tasks each day.

Monday

  1. Read the principles behind Webmaker.
  2. Set up a webmaker.org account
  3. Make #1 on this page, an introduction
  4. Log into Discourse, our discussion forum
  5. Share your introduction
  6. Ask questions and discuss the reading together!

Tuesday

  1. Read about Building on the Web
  2. Make #2 on this page, a quick educational resource
  3. Share and discuss the reading and your work

Wednesday

  1. Read about Purpose + Mix + Play + Share
  2. Discuss the reading

Thursday

  1. Read Participate, Share, Grow
  2. Discuss a "Networked Community"

Friday

  1. Find a Mozillian: Mozillians is a community directory where you can find kindred spirits in your area. This directory allows us to self organize, find each other and know each others interests.
  2. Find a Mozilla Rep: Mozilla Reps are a group of highly active volunteers who advance the Mozilla mission and are models of open behavior. They are always willing to help, and know a great deal about the Mozilla Community and openness.
  3. Find an interesting page on the wiki: The Mozilla wiki has information and documentation on all the things Mozilla is working on. Some wiki pages are updated more than others, but for Webmaker especially, this is a great place to find out about various initiatives and roadmaps.
  4. Bookmark: Discourse is a place to discuss all things teaching and learning around the digital world. We invite you to continue to use it and can help you organize information within it too :)
  5. If you haven't explored the Webmaker site yet, please do.
  6. Ask us anything! Post questions, concerns, queries and thoughts to Discourse. What did you find? What do you not understand? What was interesting?

Assessment and review

  • Discussion questions: What are you looking forward to in the live training? What is the value of participatory learning? What seems hard about it? How can you do this with lots of people? What does "Networked Community" mean for our organizations?
  • Peer Assessment: Look at one another's work and give each other feedback. Hold each other accountable for participation, and encourage open discussion with one another.
  • Sharing. If you use twitter, use the #TeachTheWeb hashtag to share what you're making and doing. Otherwise, post and respond in Discourse.

What you'll make together

We explore the Webmaker ecosystem, set up our user accounts and tinker around with tools to make introductions and lightweight open educational resources. We'll spend most of our time reflecting on our discussions.

Learning objectives

Be able to:

  • Recognize making as learning
  • Recognize connected learning
  • Recognize the attributes of the Open Web
  • Use Webmaker tools for self expression
  • Use Webmaker tools to create OERs
  • Log into Webmaker
  • Use Discourse for discussing topics

Understand that:

  • We can add the teaching and learning of web literacy competencies into any subject or topic.
  • Webmaking intermingles social skills, technical skills and topical knowledge.

The in-person workshop

Over the course of two days, we'll use this Training Agenda to explore participatory learning and how to #TeachTheWeb.
