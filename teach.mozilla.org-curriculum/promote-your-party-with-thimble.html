Activity

Promote your party with Thimble

Learners use HTML to remix and create their own event invitation and promotional page!

Made by laura

Steps for the Activity

  1. Review the Thimble project on Webmaker.org, click the Remix button at the top and explain to learners that they will be working through the comments in the left side of the editor.

  2. Allow learners to work individually or together. Have them think about the necessary details on a promotional page, and integrate their brainstorming (from a separate activity).

  3. Float around to help learners that get stuck. Allow them to play with Thimble and get used to the interface.

  4. Have learners publish their projects and post them to a blog or save links in an Etherpad. Talk about sharing. Have you posted your work to the Webmaker Community? Did you tweet with the #teachtheweb hashtag? Did you add your event to the Webmaker Event platform? Are you a member of the Webmaker Newsgroup?

Discussion

Have participants talk about their events and display their pages. Ask them what they had to do to create their project. Reinforce the idea that in HTML, elements have opening and closing tags. Ask learners why syntax and web standards are important.

Following the presentations, facilitators should direct participants to other resources in their community or online to learn more about hacking and web design/development. Provide individual constructive feedback during presentations.

Materials

  • Computer
  • Web Access
  • Projector
  • A blog or Etherpad for posting work and/or links
  • Crash Course in HTML and CSS
  • HTML Cheatsheet
  • CSS Basic Cheatsheet
  • Designing for the Web: Page Elements
