Activity

Making Learner Profiles

Learners use paper prototyping, HTML to remix and define who they want to teach and the individual needs of each type of learner.

Made by Laura

Steps for the Activity

  1. Explain why we want to define learner profiles - knowing who your audience is will help you outline how and what to teach them. You can also use the Designing for the Web: Planning resource to help learners think about defining audience.

  2. Ask learners to work together to talk about their learners and create profiles for each kind. Encourage people to use paper and markers, create mindmaps, and draw their learner profiles out.

  3. After 15 or 20 minutes, circle up and have each group take about their paper learner profiles. Ask them what they had to do to agree on each type of learner.

  4. Introduce the Thimble project on Webmaker.org. Explain that learners will remix this page to be about the people they would like to teach.

  5. Float around to help learners that get stuck. Allow them to play with Thimble and get used to the interface.

  6. Have learners publish their projects and post them to a blog or save links in an Etherpad.

Discussion

Have participants talk about and display their pages. Ask them if their learner profiles changed at all once they started documenting the profiles digitally.

Following the presentations, facilitators should direct participants to other resources in their community or online to learn more about determining user groups and web design/development. Provide individual constructive feedback during presentations.
