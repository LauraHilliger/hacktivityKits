Activity

Reflecting on Facilitation with Popcorn

Learners plan and create an interactive film to reflect on what they've learned about facilitation and create a digital story around their future aspirations.

Made by Laura

Steps for the Activity

  1. Collectively explore the facilitation of a participatory learning experience.

    Leading a discussion around facilitation can help learners understand that:

    • The ultimate goal of the event is to strengthen a community, and engender collaboration and dialog that sustain long after the live event.
    • Participatory events have malleable agendas, facilitators should pay attention to their participants, and shift plans based on feedback. Using part of the first morning to let participants discuss the agenda, and request additional sessions to be offered is always a good idea.
    • Sessions are designed to be highly interactive. You won't see any panels or keynotes at Local events, and presentations will be short and intended to spur discussion.
    • Facilitators understand that their primary goal is to enable learning, address questions, and support making and peer sharing. Participants should feel invited to bring questions and knowledge to share!

  2. Break into groups: Have groups make a list or a drawing of what their films will include. Have them think about these questions and make a plan:

    • What have you noticed about the way we've been running this training?
    • What connections have you made?
    • What do you think the goal of this event is?

    Brainstorm what your film will be.

  3. Start building! Have learners find video footage that can serve as the visuals for their new stories (try the Prelinger Archives for archival video footage, or use YouTube)

  4. Have learners use Popcorn Maker to create an interactive video. Allow them to collaborate to figure out Popcorn Maker and tinker around with their own ideas.

Materials

  • Markers
  • Paper
  • Computer
  • Web Access
  • Etherpad

Discussion

Have participants talk about their films and display the work they have done. Following the presentations, facilitators should direct participants to other resources in their community or online to learn more about filmmaking and remixing. Talk about changing the context of something by adding additional information. Provide individual constructive feedback during presentations.
