What is TASCasaurus?

Introduction

This kit is a remix of a remix! The joint project of TASC, the Hive Learning Network NYC and Mozilla, was a remix of the original Hackasaurus curriculum. The curriculum provides educators in expanded learning time, after-school and other out-of-school time settings with a free, engaging, web-based model to teach kids how to move from digital consumers to active web producers.

Background

TASCasaurus is a partnership between TASC, a nonprofit intermediary organization, and Hive Learning Network NYC and Mozilla, the two creators of Hackasaurus. Our goal is to:

  1. Prepare and motivate community educators to deliver technology-enabled, student-driven learning opportunities using the XRay Goggles and other Webmaker tools.
  2. Use technology to engage youth in a project that will increase both their digital literacy and interest in STEM (science, technology, engineering, and mathematics). A pilot project was implemented in six New York City after-school programs to engage kids in 4th to 8th grades to re-mix websites and research STEM content. The Tascasaurus curriculum was a result of that collaboration. This kit is a remix of that curriculum.

What is STEM?

“STEM” is an acronym of science, technology, engineering, and mathematics. It encompasses a wide array of topics and professional fields. An official list of STEM disciplines does not exist, but the U.S. Immigration and Customs Enforcement lists disciplines including:

  • Physics
  • Actuarial Science
  • Chemistry
  • Mathematics
  • Statistics
  • Computer Science
  • Psychology
  • Biochemistry
  • Robotics
  • Computer Engineering
  • Electrical Engineering
  • Electronics
  • Mechanical Engineering
  • Industrial Engineering
  • Civil Engineering
  • Aerospace Engineering
  • Chemical Engineering
  • Astrophysics
  • Astronomy
  • Optics
  • Nuclear Physics
  • Mathematical Biology
  • Operations Research
  • Neurobiology
  • Biomechanics
  • Bioinformatics
  • Acoustical Engineering
  • Geographic information Systems
  • Atmospheric Sciences
  • Nanotechnology

The National Science Teachers Association (NSTA) explains that “STEM education is an interdisciplinary approach to learning where rigorous academic concepts are coupled with real-world lessons as students apply science, technology, engineering, and mathematics in contexts that make connections between school, community, work, and the global enterprise enabling the development of STEM literacy and with it the ability to compete in the new economy.”

The United States Department of Education is encouraging more students, teachers, and practitioners to specialize in the STEM fields. NSTA states that “American industries need more workers in these fields due to an aging workforce and an increasingly innovative world market.”

TASCasaurus aims to bridge the gap between STEM education and professional applications through hands-on engagement and fun activities.
