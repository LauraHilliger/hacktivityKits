Discovering the Open Web

Hacktivity Kit

Contents

The Discovering the Open Web kit uses open inquiry and a lot of post-it notes to help people learn about openness. This kit serves as an introduction to the Open Movement and the Open Web by having learners explore open projects and their own relationships to the Open Web.

View other kits! View all hacktivities!

Big Picture

  1. Participants will understand that:
    • Collaboration makes ideation more fun and successful.
    • the Web is made out of building blocks that they can rearrange to create new things through an iterative process.
    • The difference between sites on the open web and closed gardens of content
    • There are many different open projects and open organizations
    • HTML is a language used to tell computers how to build webpages so that humans can consume them
    • CSS means cascading style sheets
    • Parts of a webpage include HTML, CSS, graphic assets and text content
    • There are resources on the web for webmaking
    • What information is public on the web
    Learners will be able to :
    • Organize large amounts of data ;)
    • Create links, lists and other HTML elements using Thimble.
    • Identify the tenets of openness.
    • create layouts using HTML and CSS
    • Use keyboard shortcuts (i.e. for copying and pasting)
    • Right-click and scroll with the mouse

  2. The Results:

    • The Discovering the Open Web kit helps learners understand what the tenets of “open” are through an inquiry based approach. They will plan and produce an online "travel" webpage that explores open projects while internalizing the tenets of openness.

  3. The Event Type:

    • Hack Jam

  4. Tell the story:

    Learners will make their own travel packages for "Traveling through Openness" and use the web as their platform. They will collaborate to explore what "openness" entails and use assets from the web to create a video project in collaboration with learners around the world.

  5. Circle Up Module

    • What does "open web" mean?
    • What do open projects have in common?
    • Why is community important in open projects?
    • How did feedback help you iterate and improve your project?
    • Why is it important to have open access to the Internet?
    • Why is it important that the web is designed by many individuals?
    • Why is it important for websites to invite participation?

  4. Playtesting and Feedback

    Learners share their projects.
    Is the user:
    • following the instructions?
    • getting the joke?
    • understanding the commentary?
    Have learners form groups and take notes.
    Start a discussion by having learners complete sentences.
    • I thought you did a nice job with/of ___.
    • You might want to improve ___.
    • Your idea was fun/interesting because ___.
    • What you made was ___ and it made me think/feel ____.
    Let your learners review each other's work.

    When they're finished with their reviews, ask them what next steps would be for each project. How can each project be iterated and improved upon?
