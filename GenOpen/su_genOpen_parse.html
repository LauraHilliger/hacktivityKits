Generation Open:

Parsing Ideas

Diving In

After completing the Brainstorming Hacktivity, learners focus in on a single idea to explore and build out.

Back to the overview View other kits

Steps

  1. Return to the small groups that worked together during the Brainstorming Hacktivity and have learners choose a single idea from their mindmap. Ask them to compare and contrast the different ideas. How do the different ideas tie together? What do they have in common?

  2. Have groups focus in on that idea and begin creating desired functions and assets that would allow someone to actualy build the idea.

  3. Generate more statements, drawings, and words that expand upon the idea. Work together to discuss how the "thing" on the web would function, what it would do, who would use it and what problems it would solve.

  4. Finally, present your plans to the rest of the groups. Talk about your processes and discuss new ideas that arose from the activity. Gather feedback from your peers.

Materials

  • Pens
  • Paper
  • Markers
  • Schematics and Storyboards: Be Open to your Creativity

Discussion

Discuss the process of the hacktivity and talk about the fact that planning in collaboration can lead to more new ideas. Discuss the feasibility of each idea, and then iterate to make the plans more realistic.
