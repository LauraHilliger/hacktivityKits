Generation Open:

Answer with a Newscast

Hands on Hacking

Report on your Generation Open idea by making your own bedroom broadcast or newscast. Express your answer to the Generation Open question: If you could make anything on the Web, what would it be and why?

Back to the overview View other kits

Steps

  1. Use the mindmaps from the first Hacktivity to help plan your newscast. If you are working in groups, discuss how you will explain your idea.

    Try to answer the questions:

    • What is the most basic aspect of my idea? Is it a web app? A game? A website? A communication tool? What is the *thing* I would make on the web?
    • Who is it for?
    • What problem or issue is it addressing?
    • What would it do to address this problem or issue?
  2. Write a script

    Once you have discussed overarching plans for how you will explain the idea, write a script so you know exactly what you want to say. Writing a script will help you plan the rest of the newscast by giving you a focal point. If you know what you want to say, you'll be able to find images, tweets and popups to help illustrate your point.

  3. Make a plan
    Create a Storyboard to help you outline the newscast. Think about what Popcorn events you'll use to explain your idea. Find images and links on the web that help you explain your idea.

  4. Now, follow the steps in the Webmaker project "Make a Newscast from your Bedroom" to put your newscast together.

Materials

  • Computer
  • Web Access
  • A blog or Etherpad for posting work and/or links
  • Poster Paper
  • Web cameras

Discussion

Show peers your newscast, then make it better!

Ask your peers what they think of your video. Help each other by giving feedback and improving things that were hard to understand. Provide individual constructive feedback during presentations.
