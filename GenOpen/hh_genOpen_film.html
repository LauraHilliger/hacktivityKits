Generation Open:

Answer with a Newscast

Hands on Hacking

Report on your Generation Open idea by making your own bedroom broadcast or newscast. Express your answer to the Generation Open question: If you could make anything on the Web, what would it be and why?

Back to the overview View other kits

Steps

  1. Have learners use the plans from the second Hacktivity to help plan their newscasts. If they are working in groups, have them discuss how they will explain their idea.

    Try to answer the questions:

    • What is the most basic aspect of my idea? Is it a web app? A game? A website? A communication tool? What is the *thing* I would make on the web?
    • Who is it for?
    • What problem or issue is it addressing?
    • What would it do to address this problem or issue?
  2. Write a script

    Once learners have discussed overarching plans for how they will explain the idea, tell them to write a script so they know exactly what they want to say. Writing a script will help them plan the rest of the newscast by giving them a focal point. If they know what they want to say, they'll be able to find images, tweets and popups to help illustrate their point.

  3. Make a plan
    Learners should create a Storyboard to help them outline the newscast. Have them think about what Popcorn events they will use to explain their idea. Find images and links on the web that help with this explanation.

  4. Now, learners follow the steps in the Webmaker project "Make a Newscast from your Bedroom" to put their newscasts together.

Materials

  • Computer
  • Web Access
  • A blog or Etherpad for posting work and/or links
  • Poster Paper
  • Web cameras

Discussion

Show peers your newscast, then make it better!

Have learners ask their peers what they think the idea is. Everyone should help each other by giving feedback and improving things that made ideas hard to understand. Provide individual constructive feedback during presentations.
