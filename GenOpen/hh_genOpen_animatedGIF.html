Generation Open:

Answer with an Animated GIF

Hands on Hacking

Through the creation of an animated GIF, you will be able to express your answer to the Generation Open question: If you could make anything on the Web, what would it be and why?

Back to the overview View other kits

Steps

  1. Compare and contrast the mindmaps from the first Hacktivity to zero in on an idea for the entire group. How do the different ideas tie together? What do they have in common? You may need to create a new mindmap if there are many different ideas! Decide on the idea you would like to make GIF Animations for. Or, you can all make a GIF for your own idea!

  2. Introduce the design challenge:

    Try to answer the questions:

    • What is the most basic aspect of my idea? Is it a web app? A game? A website? A communication tool? What is the *thing* I would make on the web?
    • Who is it for?
    • What problem or issue is it addressing?
    • What would it do to address this problem or issue?
  3. Plan your GIF

    Create a Storyboard to help you tell the story of your idea in this medium. Once you know what kind of imagery you want to use, decide how those images will animate. Will it be a simple slideshow that flips through a single image for each question, or will each question have multiple images. The GIF can be as simple or as complex as you want! Complex GIFs might take a bit more time, but you can tell very complicated stories in this medium.

  4. Next, follow the steps in either the "Make your own animated GIF" or "Change the world with kind and brave animated gifs" project on Webmaker to learn how to make your GIF.

Materials

  • Computer
  • Web Access
  • A blog or Etherpad for posting work and/or links
  • Poster Paper
  • GIMP
  • Cameras (optional)

Discussion

Show peers your GIF, then make it better!

Ask your peers what they think the idea is. Help each other by giving feedback and improving things that made your Generation Open idea hard to understand Provide individual constructive feedback during presentations.
