Exploring Ideas

Hacktivity Kit

Contents

The Exploring Ideas kit uses brainstorming techniques and design constraints to help learners reflect on the question: If you could make anything on the Web, what would it be and why?

View other kits! View all hacktivities!

Big Picture

  1. Participants will understand that:
    • Collaboration makes ideation more fun and successful.
    • Mindmapping is a good way to brainstorm.
    • Outlining helps you talk about concepts.
    • Planning interactive projects works best with old fashioned paper and pencil.
    Learners will be able to :
    • Formulate ideas
    • Create mindmaps, storyboards and plans.
    • Work in a team to tell a story.
    • create an animated GIF, interactive film, succinct piece of writing or a radio piece
    • Use keyboard shortcuts (i.e. for copying and pasting)
    • Right-click and scroll with the mouse

  2. The Results:

    • The Exploring Ideas kit helps learners understand how to have ideas. They will plan and produce a project that explains one of the ideas they have while learning various webmaking techniques.

  3. The Event Type:

    • Hack Jam

  4. Tell the story:

    Learners will collaborate to formulate and explain ideas through storytelling. They will be using the web as their platform.

  5. Circle Up Module

    • How does brainstorming change initial ideas?
    • How does an open brainstorming process affect the quality of ideas?
    • Why is community important in open projects?
    • How did feedback help you iterate and improve your project?
    • Why is it important to have open access to the Internet?
    • Why is it important that the web is designed by many individuals?
    • Why is it important for websites to invite participation?

  4. Playtesting and Feedback

    Learners share their projects.
    Is the user:
    • following the instructions?
    • getting the joke?
    • understanding the commentary?
    Have learners form groups and take notes.
    Start a discussion by having learners complete sentences.
    • I thought you did a nice job with/of ___.
    • You might want to improve ___.
    • Your idea was fun/interesting because ___.
    • What you made was ___ and it made me think/feel ____.
    Let your learners review each other's work.

    When they're finished with their reviews, ask them what next steps would be for each project. How can each project be iterated and improved upon?
