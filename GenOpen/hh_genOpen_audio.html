Generation Open:

Answer with Audio

Hands on Hacking

Create a recording in response to the Generation Open question: If you could make anything on the Web, what would it be and why? Then make add images, text and tweets by adding Popcorn events to it.

Back to the overview View other kits

Steps

  1. Use the mindmaps from the first Hacktivity to help you compose an audio recording as if it were being broadcast on the radio. It might be a short poem, a funny story or a short interview.

    Try to answer the questions:

    • What is the most basic aspect of my idea? Is it a web app? A game? A website? A communication tool? What is the *thing* I would make on the web?
    • Who is it for?
    • What problem or issue is it addressing?
    • What would it do to address this problem or issue?
  2. Introduce the design challenge:

    Design Challenge: How does imagery influence the way someone hears or reads a piece of text? Now that you've composed a poem, narrative or other piece for an audio recording, you'll find web content and images to illustrate your point. If you haven't already, record your text and upload the audio file to Soundcloud. If you need a software to record and edit audio files, try Audacity. If you need help using Audacity, YouTube has lots of videos on the subject.

    You might want to create a plan for your video using pens and paper. What assets will you collect? What images will you use? Try drawing sketches, storyboards and/or plans of your videos.

  3. Remix the Radio Rookies Stop and Frisk project using Popcorn Maker to put your audio and assets together. Swap the Media Source out with your uploaded Soundcloud file. Use events to illustrate your idea.

  4. Presentation and peer review. Give and receive feedback on the juxtaposition of sound and imagery.

Materials

  • Computer
  • Web Access
  • Soundcloud account
  • Recording Software (such as Audacity)
  • Popcorn Maker
  • A blog or Etherpad for posting work and/or links
  • Poster Paper
  • Markers
  • Camera and/or computers with image editing software

Discussion

Show peers your film, then make it better!

Ask your peers what they think of your video and audio piece. Help each other by giving feedback and improving things that made your Generation Open idea hard to understand. Provide individual constructive feedback during presentations.
