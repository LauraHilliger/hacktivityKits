Type of Activity

Create plug n' play activities so that learners can explore the learning objectives you set. Try to create multiple activities in a way that leads to deeper understanding. For example, start by introducing a topic and get people thinking through a fun, collaborative activity. Then create an activity to let learners get used to processes or tools through a tutorial or explanation. Use a practical project activity to pull it all.

Steps:

  1. An introductory activity gets the blood pumping, forms connections between learners, and introduces a topic. There are thousands of different ways to introduce a topic and get learners interacting with each other. Choose one and hack it to be your own!

  2. A good second activity is one that gives pointed instruction to participants on tools or procedures. An example would be allowing the participants to get used to the interface of Thimble or Popcorn by having them make projects. Before learners can practically apply new skills, they need to be comfortable with the tools they'll be using and have a basic understanding of the skills they'll be sharpening.

  3. A practical, project based activity allows learners to delve deeper into the essential questions of your session. Go back to thinking of the results: what will your learners MAKE when they are done? What research do they need to do? What text, images, videos, sound clips do they need to gather?

  4. Make sure and find or create any resources your learners might need on creating content. Create a project that gives learners enough time to explore and tinker while working towards the completion of the activity.
