Activity

Paper Appmaking

This activity, centered on brainstorming and ideation in an offline environment, will lead participants through the process of prototyping a mobile app with a printed canvas on paper.

Made by Laura and Bobby

Steps for the Activity

  1. Start an Etherpad

    Start an Etherpad and provide participants with a link to it. Copy and paste these notes into the pad so that learners can refer to them often, and encourage them to add their own ideas:

    Strategies for encouraging donations:

    • reduce the number of form fields and the amount of typing required to make a donation
    • consider providing the ability to share a donation with friends and family
    • show how a donation will be used (e.g. "You donation will go towards providing six new computers...")
    • help people feel like they are part of a bigger movement (e.g. "We've raised $200,000 with your help!")
    • connect to celebrities or broader campaign leadership

  2. [5 mins] Break into Groups

    No one has to work alone! Try to keep your groups to 5 people or less, so that each participant has a chance to share their thoughts. Give each group a stack of post-it notes and a marker or pen.

  3. [30 mins] Brainstorming

    Encourage the groups to do a mindmapping exercise to outline their thoughts about who their audience is and what the goals of their app are.

    Provide multi-colored markers, post-it notes, old magazines, scrap paper and other creative materials to help groups brainstorm. Encourage them to jot down free-form ideas, concepts, audiences and objectives for an app. Tell them to feel free to cut out shapes, build user profiles, create wireframes for layout, and anything else that helps them envision their apps.

  4. [1.25 hour] Content Designing

    When the group is ready, distribute multiple copies of the phone template (JPG PNG PDF) to each group. Have them spend an hour wireframing the interface and content for their apps. Help them think about the flow of the app (how would a user navigate and what would they see), and draw arrows between different screens to indicate the paths a user might take through the app.

    After figuring out the interface, designing content, and becoming comfortable with the flow of their app, participants can use the presentation board (JPG PNG PDF) to summarize their work and create a presentation on their app for the larger group.

  5. [35 mins] Curating and Sharing

    When everyone is done, try using the Speed Geek Playtesting activity to share designs and get feedback.

    Photo from a Maker Party in Vishakapatnam thanks to Vikas Reddy Burri.

  6. Rejoice!

    Everyone has collaborated to created a paper prototype for a mobile app!

Discussion

Use the presentation time to reinforce the idea of flow in mobile applications. Ask participants what was hard about designing the mobile app. Ask them if and how they view mobile apps differently now that they've designed one.
